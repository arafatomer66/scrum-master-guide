# Certification Comparison Table

*All eight Scrum Master certifications covered in this guide, side by side — structural comparison, not exact current pricing.*

Exact prices, question counts, and exam lengths change over time and vary by region and promotion. This table is built to be useful even after those numbers drift — the structural facts (who issues it, whether a course is mandatory, whether it expires) are stable; anywhere a specific figure is genuinely uncertain, it's marked for you to verify directly with the issuing body rather than stated as a fact that might be stale by the time you read it.

| Certification | Issuing Body | Prerequisite | Format | Renewal Requirement | Best For |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| [PSM I](scrum-org/psm-i.md) | Scrum.org | None. No mandatory course. | Self-study, open-book online exam on Scrum.org's own platform; multiple choice/multiple answer/true-false. ~80 questions / ~60 minutes, ~85% to pass — verify current length and pass mark on scrum.org. | None — does not expire, no renewal fee. | Anyone proving foundational Scrum knowledge fast and cheaply, with no employer sponsorship required. The default first cert for developers moving into the SM role. |
| [PSM II](scrum-org/psm-ii.md) | Scrum.org | None enforced, but PSM I-equivalent knowledge plus real SM experience is the honest floor. | Self-study, open-book online exam, same platform/format family as PSM I but denser scenario questions; ~30 questions / ~90 minutes — verify current length on scrum.org. | None — does not expire, no renewal fee. | Practicing Scrum Masters who want to prove applied, scenario-level judgment beyond rule recall. |
| [PSM III](scrum-org/psm-iii.md) | Scrum.org | None enforced, but years of varied real-world SM experience is the realistic floor — not crammable. | Self-study, open-book, includes essay-style scenario questions evaluated by a human, not just auto-graded — verify current format, length, and pass mark on scrum.org. | None — does not expire, no renewal fee. | Experienced Scrum Masters seeking the highest expert-tier proof of judgment; a capstone, not an early-career goal. |
| [CSM](scrum-alliance/csm.md) | Scrum Alliance | None, but a mandatory 2-day (typically ~16 hour) course taught by a Certified Scrum Trainer, with exam eligibility only after completing it. | Mandatory paid live/virtual course + a comparatively easier follow-on exam through Scrum Alliance. | Expires; requires renewal via SEUs (Scrum Education Units) and a renewal fee on a recurring cycle — verify current cycle length and fee on scrumalliance.org. | Those whose employer is funding training and wants the most broadly recognized entry-level name-brand credential, especially in HR/L&D systems that default to it. |
| [A-CSM](scrum-alliance/a-csm.md) | Scrum Alliance | Active CSM plus a minimum amount of real Scrum Master work experience (verify current experience requirement on scrumalliance.org). | Mandatory advanced course (live/virtual, Certified Scrum Trainer or equivalent) + assessment. | Expires; requires renewal via SEUs and a fee, same family as CSM. | Practicing Scrum Masters on the Scrum Alliance track who want a mid-tier advanced credential and more depth than CSM without going all the way to CSP-SM. |
| [CSP-SM](scrum-alliance/csp-sm.md) | Scrum Alliance | Active A-CSM (or equivalent accumulated SEUs/experience per Scrum Alliance's path rules) plus substantial real-world SM experience. | Application-and-experience-based credential built on accumulated advanced training and SEUs, not a single standardized exam — verify current path requirements on scrumalliance.org. | Expires; requires renewal via SEUs and a fee, same family as CSM/A-CSM. | The Scrum Alliance track's capstone credential — experienced practitioners who've invested in the full CSM → A-CSM → CSP-SM ladder. |
| [SAFe Scrum Master](safe/safe-scrum-master.md) | Scaled Agile, Inc. | None, but a mandatory instructor-led course (live/virtual, typically ~2 days) is required before the exam. | Mandatory paid course + exam through Scaled Agile's platform. | Requires renewal (typically annual) to remain "active" in Scaled Agile's community/credential system — verify current renewal terms on scaledagile.com. | Scrum Masters working (or about to work) inside an organization running SAFe — largely non-optional in SAFe-committed enterprises. |
| [SAFe Advanced Scrum Master](safe/safe-advanced-scrum-master.md) | Scaled Agile, Inc. | Generally expects prior SAFe Scrum Master credential and real experience operating as a Scrum Master inside an Agile Release Train — verify current prerequisite on scaledagile.com. | Mandatory instructor-led course + exam, same platform family as SAFe SM but deeper, ART-level content (Program Increment facilitation, Scrum of Scrums, coaching across teams). | Requires renewal (typically annual), same family as SAFe SM. | Scrum Masters advancing into senior or ART-level facilitation roles inside an established SAFe organization. |

## Reading the table

- **Format column** is the fastest way to see the core philosophical split covered in [which certification should I get](00-which-certification-should-i-get.md): Scrum.org's three tiers are self-study exams with no mandatory course; every Scrum Alliance and SAFe credential requires a paid, instructor-led course as a gate before you can even sit the assessment.
- **Renewal Requirement** is the other structural divide: Scrum.org credentials are earned once and never expire; Scrum Alliance and SAFe credentials require ongoing renewal (SEUs and fees, or annual renewal terms respectively) to stay "current."
- Treat every specific number in this table (course length, question counts, timeboxes, renewal cycles) as approximate and **verify current details on the issuing body's own site before registering or budgeting** — this table's lasting value is the structural comparison, not any figure that's likely to drift over the life of this guide.

For the full reasoning behind which of these fits your specific situation, see [which certification should I get](00-which-certification-should-i-get.md). For Scrum.org-specific depth, start with [PSM I](scrum-org/psm-i.md) and work through the [practice questions](scrum-org/psm-practice-questions.md).
